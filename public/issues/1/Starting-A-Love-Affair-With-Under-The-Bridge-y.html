    Starting A Love Affair With ‘Under The Bridge’ y

    by Michael Farrell

    The man lies down with ‘Under The Bridge’. He puts his
    head on Auden. The other puts their head on DH Lawrence
      (They have fallen from a
    chessboard.) They are under
        a
    truck that will never reach
    Australia. Be tender: one has a long beard. The man thinks
    about having a mind. About how Auden and DH Lawrence brought
    him here. About how other songs structure feelings for the song
    you’re actually with, and how the ground becomes yet another party
      Anyone might come along: Freud might enter the picture. Some
        piece
    of chicken-shit legislation (liberalising or restricting). There was
        so much
    in Auden’s and Lawrence’s heads they’d found it difficult
    to lift them, but death had refined them and
    their minds. The man tries to not to think
    so much. ‘Under The Bridge’ appears not to be
    thinking at all. There is music: the noise of
    voices and graffiti artists. The man begins to question which Auden
    is supporting him, how early? ‘Under The Bridge’ could be acting
    out a scene. He feels as if his head is reading
    Auden in the dark. A poem titled ‘Lunch Is A Stranger’
    a Brechtian melodrama. He strokes the long beard until it
        whitens
      Don’t turn your back, they say together. Faithfulness enters the
        picture
